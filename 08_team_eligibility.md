## Why This Team Is Actually Strong for This Challenge

It would be easy to assume that an IISc research challenge wants professors and PhD students. It does not — and here is why our team is a genuine asset:

**The challenge judges are research scientists. Their problem is that research rarely reaches people.**

SANSCOG has followed 10,000 rural Karnataka patients for a decade. LASI-DAD has assessed 4,000+ Indians for dementia. And yet 80% of India's 8.8 million dementia patients remain undiagnosed. The gap is not knowledge — it is deployment.

Our team brings exactly what is missing: the skills to take research-grade AI and put it in the hands of ASHA workers. System architecture, LLM integration, product thinking, programme management — these are not secondary skills for this challenge. They are the skills that turn a research finding into a deployed tool.

The judges know this. The challenge explicitly says they want a "diverse community of innovators across academia, research, and industry." They wrote that because the last thing they need is another paper.

**We are the team they wrote that sentence for.**
