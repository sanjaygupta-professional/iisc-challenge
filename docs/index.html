The Problem

India has a brain health crisis
Western AI cannot solve

8.8 million Indians live with dementia today.
An estimated 80% are never diagnosed.

India has the world's largest type-2 diabetes population: ~101 million (ICMR-INDIAB-17, Lancet D&E 2023), with approximately 57% nationally undiagnosed. T2D is one of the strongest modifiable risk factors for Alzheimer's disease. India's dementia risk-factor profile, where education and vision impairment dominate rather than hearing loss, is structurally different from the Western profile that trained every major AI system.

MRI-based Alzheimer's tools reach roughly 0.1% of at-risk Indians. Community-level screening, using whatever data ASHA community health workers can realistically collect, is the only path to population scale. Existing AI systems trained on Western datasets (ADNI and others) systematically underperform on Indian populations.

01

World's largest T2D burden with earliest global onset. 101 million diabetics, 57% undiagnosed.

02

SANSCOG / TLSA cohort at IISc CBR: rural/urban paired longitudinal data with metabolic panels.

03

1 million+ ASHA workers nationwide, the only realistic frontline screening channel at community scale.

04

22 official languages requiring native cognitive assessment, not translation from English.

No Western team can credibly claim what we can. India is the only country that simultaneously has all four of the above. That is the proposal's thesis sentence, and it drives every architectural choice in BhāratBrain.

The Solution

BhāratBrain: three layers,
one integrated system

Five directions were scored against a 22-criterion rubric. Options 1, 5, and 3 each addressed different gaps. Combined, they score higher (~97/100) than any standalone option. BhāratBrain is that synthesis, still under stress-test this week before we lock it.

01

Input layer (Option 5)

Multilingual Mirror

Native cognitive assessment across Indian languages using IndicWav2Vec for speech-to-text in ~40 languages, LLM-based cognitive scoring via Sarvam-1 or Airavata, and Bhashini for deployment. No translation. No English as a proxy for cognition.

IndicWav2Vec Sarvam-1 Bhashini Airavata
02

Fusion model (Option 1)

Missing-Modality Fusion

Accepts whatever data ASHA can actually collect: voice recordings, basic blood records, demographics. Handles modality dropout, because some patients have HbA1c data and some don't. Outputs a calibrated risk score with confidence bounds.

Modality dropout Gated multimodal SANSCOG data
03

Deployment (Options 1 + 3)

ASHA Companion + Federated Flywheel

ASHA workers as the frontline screening channel. Phase 1: cross-silo federated learning across 3-5 PHCs and CBR referral sites (not cross-device smartphones). T2D metabolic risk as a weighted input channel, flagging diabetic patients as higher-priority for screening. Cold-start seed model trained on SANSCOG historical data.

Flower FL NVIDIA FLARE Cross-silo Phase 1

Known gaps being stress-tested this week

  • A1 FL must be cross-silo (PHC-to-PHC), not cross-device. No published health FL runs at ASHA phone scale yet. Revising Phase 1 architecture.
  • A2 Ground truth is undefined. "85% accuracy" at what, on which validation set, adjudicated by whom? Drafting a precise falsifiable claim.
  • A3 IndicWav2Vec to Sarvam-1 to Bhashini pipeline has never been built end-to-end for cognitive assessment. Phase 1 language scope must be honest.
  • A4 Missing-modality fusion needs a specific model family. Modality dropout or gated multimodal, trained on what data shape?
