Retinal fundus photography offers a non-invasive, sub-5-minute window into brain aging. Zhu et al. (2021, UK Biobank, n≈33,000) demonstrated that an elevated Retinal Age Gap (RAG = AI-predicted retinal age minus chronological age) is prospectively associated with incident all-cause dementia (HR ≈1.25–1.31 per year of RAG). However, no RAG-dementia study has been conducted in Indian populations. Existing deep learning models were trained on predominantly white European retinas and face systematic calibration bias when applied to Indian eyes — due to higher retinal pigment epithelium (RPE) melanin — potentially miscalculating RAG by 3–7 years.

We propose RetinaAge India: using SANSCOG retinal imaging and CBR cohort data, we will (1) adapt a pre-trained retinal age deep learning model to Indian retinal characteristics via transfer learning, (2) compute calibrated RAG for SANSCOG participants with known CDR-staged cognitive status, (3) quantify the RAG–MCI/dementia association with rigorous confounder adjustment for T2D, hypertension, and diabetic retinopathy, and (4) propose a scalable screening pathway leveraging India's existing 10,000+ deployed DR screening cameras (Remidio, Forus, Aravind).

Primary novelty: First RAG-dementia association study in Indian subjects. Publishable regardless of effect direction. First Indian-population retinal age calibration model. Proposes dual-use of India's DR screening infrastructure for dementia risk — zero additional hardware cost.

2Problem Statement
▼

Three Converging Crises Motivate RetinaAge India

Crisis 1 — Dementia detection gap: 8.8 million Indians with dementia, 80% undiagnosed. PET/CSF workup costs ₹50,000–1,50,000 and is inaccessible outside 6–8 cities. A scalable, low-cost biomarker for dementia risk stratification at community level does not exist.
Crisis 2 — Fundus camera infrastructure underutilised: India has deployed 10,000+ non-mydriatic fundus cameras through NPCB (National Programme for Control of Blindness), Aravind Eye Care, Sankara Nethralaya, and Remidio/Forus health tech, primarily for diabetic retinopathy (DR) screening. These cameras are idle between DR screening cycles and could screen for dementia risk with zero additional hardware investment.
Crisis 3 — Evidence gap for Indian populations: Published RAG-dementia evidence (Zhu et al. 2021, NUS Singapore 2023) cannot be applied to Indian populations without recalibration. Indian RPE melanin is higher than in European populations, systematically reducing fundus image contrast and shifting AI age predictions. A UK Biobank model applied directly to Indian retinas may over- or under-estimate RAG by 3–7 years — rendering risk stratification unreliable.

Why the Retina Reflects Brain Health

The mechanistic link between retinal and brain aging is well-established across five pathways:

  • Embryological: Retina is derived from the diencephalon; retinal ganglion cells are true central nervous system neurons.
  • Amyloid-beta deposition: Post-mortem studies identify amyloid plaques in retinal tissue via the same APP/BACE1 cleavage pathway as cortical AD plaques.
  • Trans-synaptic neurodegeneration: Visual cortex → lateral geniculate → optic nerve → retinal ganglion cell chain allows cortical AD neurodegeneration to propagate retrogradely.
  • Shared vascular pathology: Cerebral small vessel disease in AD is mirrored in retinal microvasculature; retinal arteriolar narrowing correlates with brain white matter hyperintensities.
  • Neuroinflammation: Microglial activation, complement deposition, and tau pathology documented in retinal tissue post-mortem.

The Specific Research Gap

No published study has: (1) validated a retinal age model on Indian retinal images; (2) measured RAG-dementia association in any Indian cohort; (3) proposed dual-use of India's DR screening infrastructure for dementia risk. This study would be the first published RAG-dementia evidence for Indian populations — a novel scientific contribution regardless of whether the UK Biobank effect size replicates.

Stage 1 — Base Retinal Age Model

Pre-trained ResNet-50 or EfficientNet-B3 for retinal age regression, using open-source weights from SEED dataset (NUS Singapore; multi-ethnic Asian including Indian participants, age-labeled fundus images). Input: colour fundus photograph (RGB). Output: predicted age in years (continuous). Architecture choice validated by Poplin et al. 2018 (Nature Biomedical Engineering): MAE ~3.26 years on UK Biobank.

Stage 2 — Indian Domain Adaptation

Fine-tune Stage 1 model on SANSCOG retinal images (Indian population) via transfer learning: freeze early feature extraction layers; retrain final 2–3 layers on SANSCOG age-labeled fundus images. Measure calibration shift: compare age prediction MAE before and after domain adaptation. Target: MAE <4 years post-adaptation on SANSCOG hold-out. This corrects for Indian RPE melanin bias — a novel technical contribution in itself.

Stage 3 — Confounder Adjustment

Collect T2D status, hypertension, smoking, and DR severity (ETDRS grade 0–4) from SANSCOG data. Regress these from RAG score using linear models to compute Adjusted RAG. Critical because: diabetes → diabetic retinopathy → elevated RAG; diabetes → dementia (established). Without adjustment, RAG-dementia association may be wholly explained by shared T2D causation.

Exclude subjects with severe DR (ETDRS ≥3) from primary analysis; include them in sensitivity analysis to assess confounding magnitude.

Stage 4 — Dementia Association Analysis

  • Cross-sectional (primary): Logistic regression, Adjusted RAG vs. CDR-staged MCI/dementia status in SANSCOG. OR per year of RAG; 95% CI.
  • Longitudinal (if available): Cox proportional hazard model using SANSCOG follow-up waves. HR per year RAG for incident dementia — analogous to Zhu et al. 2021 design.
  • Incremental value: Compare C-statistic of Age + T2D + HTN base model vs. base model + Adjusted RAG. If ΔC-statistic >0.03, RAG adds clinically meaningful predictive value.

Stage 5 — Risk Output & Screening Pathway

Community eye camp workflow: Fundus photo (<5 min, technician-level) → RAG score computed → Low/Medium/High risk tier → if Medium/High → RUDAS cognitive screen administered → if RUDAS elevated → PHC referral for physician assessment.

Integration pathway with ScreenBrain (Proposal 1): RetinaAge provides the community-level triage layer; ScreenBrain provides the confirmatory cognitive assessment layer. Together: a two-stage dementia risk pathway deployable at DR screening camps with zero additional infrastructure.

Acknowledged Limitations

  • RAG is not AD-specific: Elevated RAG reflects all causes of retinal aging (diabetes, hypertension, smoking, genetics). Dementia subtype specificity unknown.
  • CDR ground truth: CDR staging ≠ biomarker-confirmed AD pathology. Association findings are for all-cause dementia risk, not AD specifically.
  • Cross-sectional primary analysis: Cannot establish that RAG diverges before symptom onset. Prospective window unknown without longitudinal SANSCOG retinal data.
  • Confounding by shared metabolic pathway: T2D → DR → elevated RAG and T2D → dementia could explain RAG-dementia association entirely. Mediation analysis is critical.
  • Image quality degradation: Real-world Indian field conditions (cataracts, pterygium, field alignment) reduce fundus quality; 2025 ICMR evaluation found AI sensitivity 59.7–97.7% depending on camera and algorithm.

8India-First Design
▼
  • Indian retinal pigmentation makes this research uniquely India-specific: Higher RPE melanin in Indian eyes causes calibration bias in European-trained models — a technical problem no other country's team could investigate without Indian retinal data. This research can only be done in India, with Indian data.
  • NPCB DR infrastructure dual-use: 10,000+ fundus cameras deployed across India for diabetic retinopathy screening. RetinaAge India proposes that these cameras — already in position, already operated — can simultaneously screen for dementia risk. Zero additional hardware. Zero new deployment. Policy change only.
  • SANSCOG retinal imaging irreplaceable: The only rural Karnataka cohort with paired fundus images + metabolic data + CDR-staged cognitive outcomes. No Western team has access to this dataset combination.
  • High-prevalence T2D confounder analysis: India's ~11% adult T2D prevalence means diabetic retinopathy co-occurs in the same age group (50+) as dementia risk. This confounding problem is uniquely acute for India and requires India-specific analysis design (ETDRS exclusion, mediation analysis) that Western RAG studies have not needed.
  • Aravind / Remidio / Forus partnership pathway: India-headquartered DR AI companies with existing NPCB relationships and deployed fundus infrastructure. No comparable partner ecosystem exists outside India for this scale of community-level eye screening.
  • DPDPA 2023 compliance: Retinal images are biometric data under DPDPA 2023. All processing on IISc-controlled infrastructure; ABHA patient ID for longitudinal linkage; no cloud transfer without CBR approval. CDSCO Software as Medical Device (SaMD) pathway considered for Phase 2 deployment.
  • Complementarity with ScreenBrain: RetinaAge provides community-level triage (fundus photo → RAG → risk tier) as an entry point; ScreenBrain provides confirmatory cognitive assessment. A two-stage India-native dementia risk pathway — neither proposal depends on the other, but both are stronger together.
