
Idea 1: India-specific MCI-to-Dementia Progression Model Build a model using CBR-SANSCOG/TLSA longitudinal data to predict conversion from normal cognition or MCI to dementia, specifically calibrated for Indian demographics. The differentiator is training on Indian cohorts rather than applying ADNI-trained models to an Indian population.
Idea 2: Multimodal Fusion with Indian Vernacular Language Biomarkers Develop a speech/language-based cognitive screening model using Indian languages (Kannada, Telugu, Hindi). The DementiaBank dataset exists in English — there's almost nothing in Indian languages. Collect or use CBR's existing vernacular cognitive tests and build acoustic + linguistic models.
Idea 3: Low-Resource / Low-Cost Screening Pipeline Most existing models require MRI, PET scans, or expensive biomarkers — inaccessible in rural India. Build an AI model that uses only low-cost inputs: cognitive test scores, blood pressure, blood glucose, sociodemographic variables, and activity questionnaires. Validate against SANSCOG ground truth* - this is where I did more research and prepared a document.
Idea 4: Differential Risk Modeling for Education-Illiteracy Bias Cognitive tests systematically underdiagnose dementia in illiterate individuals. Build a model that accounts for literacy level as a key covariate, using education-adjusted and education-free cognitive features, validated on the LASI-DAD dataset where illiterate/innumerate-specific tests were included.
Idea 5: Genetic + Clinical Risk Score for Indian Populations Integrate APOE ?4 status with India-specific genetic variants (from the GenomeIndia project that CBR has participated in) alongside clinical features, building a polygenic risk model specifically for South Asian ancestry groups.
Idea 6: Longitudinal Brain Age Gap Model Train a "brain age estimation" model on healthy Indian aging participants (CBR-TLSA urban cohort), then compute the gap between predicted brain age and chronological age as a biomarker of neurodegeneration. This is well-established in Western research but has never been done on an Indian cohort.
Idea 7: Environmental + Lifestyle Risk Factor Interaction Model India-specific modifiable risk factors — air pollution, nutritional patterns (including micronutrient deficiencies like B12, common in vegetarian populations), sleep quality, physical activity in agricultural work — interact differently from Western risk models. Build an XGBoost or causal inference model on LASI-DAD/SANSCOG that captures these interactions.
Idea 8: Federated / Privacy-Preserving Multi-Site Model Build a federated learning framework that allows CBR, NIMHANS, and other Indian clinical sites to collaboratively train a model without sharing raw patient data. This is an infrastructure/methodology contribution addressing a critical bottleneck in Indian health AI research.
